## Brand definition
Hook the Horizon is a modern, premium, field-capable fishing and outdoor publication that connects water, weather, species, equipment, technique, preparation, evidence, stewardship, and real field judgment to a better next decision.

It is youthful but not childish; adventurous but not theatrical; technically capable without becoming a sterile dashboard; visually rich without clutter; premium without black-and-gold imitation; and explicit about source, freshness, uncertainty, safety, regulation, and location privacy.

## Positioning, voice, and boundaries
Hook owns fishing/outdoor field intelligence: species, habitat, water/weather relationships, equipment compatibility, rigging and presentation, technique, preparation, evidence, uncertainty, stewardship, and actionable exploration. It is not a tournament-aggression brand, trophy gallery, secret-location directory, regulation authority, guaranteed-catch service, generic outdoor lifestyle publication, or detached SaaS product.

The voice is an intelligent younger field editor: direct, capable, technically fluent, responsible, curious, and lightly humorous. Never fabricate catches, tests, field visits, product use, current conditions, regulations, species presence, compatibility, or sensitive coordinates.

## Visual, content, and interface system
Use cinematic open water and horizon, weather, depth, reflection, equipment, boats, species and underwater detail, restrained maps/contours/ripples, field materials, asymmetrical composition, compact technical modules, and deliberate dark/light transitions.

Governing page rhythm: immersion → orientation → evidence → explanation → decision → related exploration → visual rest.

Every substantial item identifies the reader job, field context, source/freshness, environmental/species/equipment relationships, assumptions, uncertainty, safety/regulation/stewardship boundaries, and next action. Required distinctions include measured, observed, sourced, manufacturer-stated, estimated, inferred, editorial judgment, unknown, stale, and location-sensitive.
