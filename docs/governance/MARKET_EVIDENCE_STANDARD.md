# Hook the Horizon Market Evidence Standard

**Status:** P0 active governing authority  
**Effective date:** 2026-07-15  
**Applies to:** Topic selection, species priorities, technique priorities, product categories, Gear Verdicts, Field Reports, Water Profiles, tools, classes, and launch planning.

## Governing rule

Market evidence determines what earns coverage. Official records establish factual and regulatory baselines. Fieldwork determines how Hook the Horizon tests and differentiates the selected subject. Editorial opinion shapes presentation only after those layers are documented.

Personal preference, anecdotal popularity, retailer merchandising, contributor success, or a single field session may not be presented as proof of market demand, category size, typical performance, or industry preference.

## Evidence hierarchy

1. Government and regulatory records for participation, licenses, expenditures, fishery surveys, stocking, regulations, closures, access, and conservation facts.
2. Recognized industry research with disclosed methods, including RBFF, American Sportfishing Association, Outdoor Industry Association/Foundation, and comparable category authorities.
3. Platform-demand data such as Google Trends, Google Ads Keyword Planner, Search Console after launch, YouTube, Pinterest, and comparable sources. Record geography, period, query type, and retrieval date.
4. Commercial and competitive indicators such as retailer category depth, manufacturer breadth, price bands, affiliate availability, advertiser evidence, publisher coverage, and SERP competition. These support but do not independently prove market size.
5. Field and audience evidence such as documented tests, reader questions, internal search, newsletter behavior, tool use, and post-launch analytics. These refine treatment but do not establish original market size by themselves.

## Required measurements

Every market-led priority must record, where available:

- Participation or audience size and direction
- Segment relevance
- Search demand and trend direction
- Commercial value
- Problem frequency and practical utility
- Competitive supply and content-quality gaps
- Regional access and repeatability
- Seasonality
- Evidence date, geography, methodology, and limitations

## Quantified scoring model

Score each candidate from 0 to 5 on each factor. Multiply each factor score by its weight, sum the results, then multiply by 20 to convert to the 0–100 scale used in the decision thresholds below.

| Factor | Weight |
|---|---:|
| Participation and audience size | 20% |
| Search demand and trend direction | 20% |
| Commercial value | 15% |
| Problem frequency and practical utility | 15% |
| Competitive opportunity | 10% |
| Regional access and repeatability | 10% |
| Editorial fit | 5% |
| Evidence quality and freshness | 5% |

Decision thresholds:

- 75-100: launch priority
- 60-74: supporting launch content
- 45-59: research backlog
- Below 45: do not prioritize

A subject cannot score above 59 unless participation or search demand is supported by a quantifiable source. A subject cannot receive launch-priority status when its evidence-quality score is below 3.

## Publication controls

- Do not call a subject popular, growing, best-selling, standard, most used, or preferred without a dated quantifiable source.
- Do not generalize a field observation into an industry conclusion.
- Do not present retailer assortment as market share.
- Do not present search-volume estimates without geography, period, source, and retrieval date.
- Do not produce a Gear Verdict from market data alone. Market data may select the category; verified ownership and field use support the verdict.
- Do not treat a planned setup, a manufacturer specification, or a popular product as an owned or tested product.
- Mark all unresolved decisions as provisional.

## Candidate comparison requirement

Before locking launch priorities, compare at minimum:

- Species: rainbow trout, smallmouth bass, largemouth bass, walleye, crappie, and catfish.
- Techniques: bank fishing, spinning, finesse, fly fishing, trolling, jigging, topwater, and bait fishing.
- Equipment: spinning reels, rods, line and leader, lures, tackle storage, electronics, waders, and safety equipment.
- Reader problems: compatibility, knot failure, casting, retrieve control, seasonal fish location, water clarity, weather, access, regulations, and fish handling.
- Water Profiles: Chatfield plus at least four comparable Colorado public waters.

## Relationship to field evidence

Market data selects the question. Official sources establish the baseline. Field evidence tests the question under disclosed conditions. The young editor's firsthand experience supplies judgment, observations, photographs, corrections, and voice. None of those layers may be substituted for another.

## Required output

The next topic-locking artifact must include raw values, normalized scores, weighted totals, confidence ratings, source citations, retrieval dates, geographic scope, and explicit limitations.
