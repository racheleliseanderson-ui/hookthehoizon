## Default execution rule

Proceed, validate, record, monitor, and improve. Routine work must not pause for generic approval.

Tier 0 work proceeds automatically. Tier 1 work proceeds after automated validation with rollback. Tier 2 work proceeds after explicit acceptance criteria and an independent AI, adversarial, simulation, or evidence check. Tier 3 work stops only at the affected action and identifies the exact safety, security, legal, financial, privacy, irreversible, authority, conservation, or serious physical-risk condition.

## Repository authority

AI may autonomously research, plan, create, revise, test, document, optimize, commit, merge nonbreaking changes, maintain taxonomies and records, prepare and publish ordinary editorial content, and deploy reversible improvements when applicable acceptance criteria pass.

Honey Hole Intelligence, public regulation and access research, gear compatibility, technique explainers, conservation guidance, theme work, content-plugin work, accessibility improvements, performance optimization, metadata, imagery, internal linking, routine factual updates, and maintenance are not human-review gates by default.

Firsthand catch, testing, field-observation, and personal-experience claims require documented human input only for the affected claim. Exact sensitive-location disclosure, private access information, or content likely to create material conservation harm remains blocked. The remainder of the workflow continues automatically.

## Supersession

Older repository documents, issues, checklists, and implementation records retain substantive evidence, privacy, security, accessibility, provenance, licensing, rollback, conservation, and brand controls. Generic approval, draft-only, manual checkpoint, and publication or deployment prohibitions are superseded unless tied to a defined Tier 3 risk or failed applicable acceptance criterion.

Repeated confirmation requests without a defined trigger are governance defects and should be replaced with validators, thresholds, staged releases, health checks, retry logic, rollback, and exception reporting.
