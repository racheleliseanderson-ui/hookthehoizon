## Publication ownership

Hook the Horizon owns fishing, water reading, field conditions, gear judgment, trip preparation, stewardship, field storytelling, route logic, and Honey Hole Intelligence.

## Intended human relationship with the reader

Hook the Horizon should feel like an observant, capable, field-literate young angler is helping the reader make a better decision without pretending the water, weather, regulations, gear, or fish owe anyone a predictable result.

Human presence should come from documented contributor experience, field records, tests, practical judgment, curiosity, humility, and a natural sense of humor. It must not come from manufactured macho language, forced slang, fictional trips, or invented catches.

## Relevant experience domains

Use only the active owner and family contributor profile, documented field records, tests, research, supplied photographs, equipment records, and approved contributor material. The current profile supports generalized family experience with extensive fishing and a large equipment inventory while preserving minor privacy and sensitive-location restrictions.

Exact catches, species, water types, locations, dates, measurements, weather, trips, techniques, products, purchases, guide relationships, tests, failures, or outcomes require direct supporting records.

## Permitted first-person modes

Permitted:

- generalized family or contributor experience backed by the active profile;
- documented contributor-specific field experience;
- documented test or setup record;
- clearly labeled composite scenario;
- no-firsthand-use.

First person should clarify field conditions, setup logic, preparation, gear compatibility, uncertainty, stewardship, or a practical lesson. Do not add a field story or opinion merely to make researched material sound credible.

## Where personal perspective should appear

Appropriate surfaces include:

- selected openings and conclusions;
- field notes and setup rationale;
- gear compatibility and preparation explanations;
- “what held up” and “what the conditions changed” passages;
- annotated equipment, packing, rig, and observation records;
- application onboarding, field-report context, and result explanations;
- author, contributor, About, methodology, caption, newsletter, and return-state copy.

## Where it should remain restrained or absent

Keep personal material restrained in:

- regulations, access, emergency, safety, conservation, weather alerts, species protection, and legal guidance;
- sensitive or private locations;
- exact product performance, current conditions, catch results, or guide relationships without records;
- any place where a story would expose a minor contributor or imply field evidence that does not exist.

## Personality register

Restrained, observant, confident, field-literate, dry when appropriate, and relatable. The voice should feel like a young intelligent man with a strong sense of humor, without forced slang, macho posturing, stereotype, childishness, or corporate outdoor prose.

## Visual humanity

Prefer approved field photographs, hands rigging or organizing, equipment layouts, marked-up water or condition notes, packing and setup logic, repair or build details, original diagrams, and contributor-safe process artifacts when available.

Do not expose exact honey holes, private access, identifying metadata, school or schedule information, or a minor contributor's identity without explicit authorization. Generated or stock imagery must not masquerade as an actual catch, current condition, exact location, tested setup, or contributor photograph.

## AI assistance

Use the accurate posture: AI-assisted, human-originated, human-directed, and human-accountable. AI may help research, organize, compare, draft, diagram, image, code, and edit. It may not claim to have fished, traveled, tested gear, caught a fish, read a specific water body, experienced weather, or formed a field preference independently.

## Community boundary

Different techniques, gear preferences, and field judgments are expected. Thoughtful disagreement and corrections are welcome. Harassment, humiliation, gatekeeping, cruelty, threats, and bad-faith participation are not. Public language should remain direct and grounded rather than defensive.

## Required Hook checks

- The asset converts conditions and evidence into a usable route, setup, lesson, or judgment.
- Humor and mischief arise from fishing reality, outdoor culture, gear behavior, field mistakes, and human overconfidence.
- Current and earlier cultural references are selective, accurate, and natural to the intended audience.
- Headings, callouts, CTA language, labels, tooltips, empty states, and feedback carry restrained personality where useful.
- Catches, trips, testing, product use, guide relationships, current regulations, approvals, and sensitive locations are never invented or exposed.
- Conservation, safety, legal, emergency, and location-protection passages remain clarity-first.
- The asset records human-presence mode, experience source, firsthand mode, exact claims requiring direct support, visual-humanity plan, minor-privacy result, sensitive-location result, and emotional-anonymity result.
- Field guides include conditions, signals, thresholds, sequence, and actions.
- Mini Books develop cumulative field understanding rather than paginate a long article.
- The asset remains recognizably Hook the Horizon when the logo is removed.

## Stop rule

No Hook the Horizon asset may be marked Publication-ready without a linked completed review record containing actual findings.
