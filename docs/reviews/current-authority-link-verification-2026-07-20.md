## Findings

- Thunder is an immediate shelter trigger. A substantial building or enclosed metal-topped vehicle is the relevant general shelter, and the NWS uses a 30-minute post-thunder wait rule.
- Cold-water risk includes cold shock, involuntary gasping, rapid breathing, cognitive impairment, physical incapacitation, hypothermia, and drowning. Air temperature is not a substitute for water-temperature planning.
- Heat, flood, wildfire, wind, thunderstorm, visibility, and road or evacuation conditions can independently end a fishing plan. Local warnings and the safe return route remain controlling.
- USGS measurements are provisional observations and trends. A gauge does not establish that an exact crossing, bank, ice surface, wade, launch, or boat route is safe.
- Fishing regulation ownership normally starts with the responsible state or tribal authority and can be supplemented by federal, park, facility, municipal, land-manager, emergency, boating, and special-use restrictions.
- CPW identifies its online fishing brochure as the most current Colorado brochure. Colorado also requires professional inspection for specified motorized, sail, and trailered watercraft and can impose water-specific closures and drought controls.
- Public-land ownership maps do not prove every legal access route. Private, tribal, trust, leased, or restricted land requires the applicable authority or permission. The full parking-to-water-to-exit route must be established.
- Clean, Drain, Dry is a baseline. Inspection, professional decontamination, transport, bait, drain-plug, seal, receipt, and equipment rules can be stricter locally.
- General fish-handling authorities support keeping fish wet, minimizing air exposure, using suitable wet equipment, and avoiding damaging hook removal. Species, temperature, depth, invasive status, and local law can require a different or no-release decision.
- Trout temperature guidance varies by jurisdiction and fishery. A state advisory can support that state’s decision framework but must not be converted into a universal national threshold.
- Guide, charter, and lodge evaluation requires current operator terms, trip specification, cancellation and safety policies, accessibility, applicable state or land-manager authorization, and Coast Guard credential or inspected-vessel checks when relevant. Reviews are context, not proof.
- An OUPV credential is generally associated with uninspected passenger vessels carrying six or fewer passengers for hire. Larger passenger-for-hire operations may require a master credential and a current Certificate of Inspection; the exact vessel and operation control.
- TSA permits fishing rods subject to carrier limits and directs large or dangerous hooks toward secure checked-baggage handling. The operating airline and destination rules also control.

## Immediate pre-publication gate

Any publication involving a named water, access route, current regulation, closure, weather or water condition, passenger-for-hire operator, vessel, land manager, or species-handling protocol requires a new same-cycle check of:

1. the exact jurisdiction, water, parcel, operator, vessel, activity, and date;
2. current fish and wildlife rules and water-specific exceptions;
3. current land-manager, access, road, launch, fire, and closure information;
4. current NWS forecast, warnings, and relevant hazard products;
5. current water, release, flow, temperature, tide, or facility source and its timestamp;
6. current operator terms, credential, permit, concession, or vessel inspection where applicable;
7. current handling, invasive-species, bait, transport, and conservation requirements; and
8. location-privacy and contributor-safety review.

If the exact local context is not supplied or cannot be established, the content remains draft, the disputed instruction is removed or generalized, and no readiness or publication claim is permitted.

## Editorial rule

Link the authority responsible for the exact decision. Keep review dates and limitations adjacent. Never imply direct confirmation, legal access, safe conditions, operator approval, current availability, or a permanent rule unless that evidence exists.
