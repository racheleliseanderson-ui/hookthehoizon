## Findings

- Thunder is an immediate shelter trigger; a substantial building or enclosed metal-topped vehicle is the relevant general route, and the NWS uses a 30-minute post-thunder wait rule.
- Cold-water risk includes cold shock, involuntary gasping, rapid breathing, incapacitation, hypothermia, and drowning; air temperature is not a substitute for water-temperature planning.
- USGS measurements are provisional observations and trends. A gauge does not establish that an exact crossing, bank, ice surface, wade, or boat route is safe.
- Fishing regulation ownership normally starts with the responsible state or tribal authority and can be supplemented by federal, park, facility, municipal, or land-manager restrictions.
- Public-land ownership maps do not prove every legal access route. Private-land crossing requires permission, and current closures and local rules remain controlling.
- Clean, Drain, Dry is a baseline; inspection, decontamination, transport, bait, and equipment rules can be stricter locally.
- TSA permits fishing rods subject to carrier limits and directs large or dangerous hooks toward secure checked-baggage handling. The operating airline and destination rules also control.
- Guide/charter/lodge evaluation requires current operator terms, cancellation and safety policies, applicable state/business licensing, and Coast Guard credential or inspected-vessel checks when relevant. Reviews are context, not proof.

## Editorial rule

Link the authority responsible for the exact decision. Keep review dates and limitations adjacent. Never imply direct confirmation, legal access, safe conditions, operator approval, current availability, or a permanent rule unless that evidence exists.
