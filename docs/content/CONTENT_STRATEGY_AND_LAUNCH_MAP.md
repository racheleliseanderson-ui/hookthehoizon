## 2. Content decision record

### HTH-CONTENT-001 — Resolve the launch-count conflict

The blueprint describes an “18-piece starter library,” but its itemized launch list totals 19 pieces. For the bounded v1 library, combine the **Start Here page** and the **contributor methodology/evidence page** into one substantial orientation page. This preserves every required subject while maintaining the stated 18-piece scope.

### HTH-CONTENT-002 — Lead with decisions, not chronology

The initial library is organized around the decisions readers need to make:

1. Read the water and conditions.
2. Choose or adjust a technique.
3. Build a compatible equipment system.
4. Understand the target species.
5. Evaluate field evidence and gear claims.
6. Plan a water or destination responsibly.

### HTH-CONTENT-003 — Use a small durable content model

The first release uses only the approved bounded post types:

- Field Report
- Gear Review
- Species Profile
- Technique
- Water/Destination Profile

Water-reading guides and equipment-system guides can launch as structured standard articles. Comparisons, rigs, guide/lodge profiles, and tools remain templates or relationships until content volume justifies a separate post type.

## 3. Editorial promise

**Hook the Horizon helps readers interpret conditions, match equipment, improve technique, and make better decisions on the water through transparent field experience, current sources, visible limitations, and practical alternatives.**

Every article must:

- Solve a defined reader problem.
- Separate observation, inference, official information, and recommendation.
- State the experience or evidence basis.
- Show limitations, compatibility, alternatives, and a non-purchase path.
- Display review dates for regulations, access, prices, specifications, and availability.
- Apply location-privacy controls before publication.
- Complete the fishing answer before any sister-publication handoff.

## 7. Production sequence and dependencies

### Wave 1 — Establish trust and the decision spine

Publish or stage HTH-001, HTH-002, HTH-003, HTH-006, and HTH-009 first. These pieces define the site promise and supply the core internal-link routes for the rest of the library.

### Wave 2 — Complete the practical authority layer

Produce HTH-004, HTH-005, HTH-007, HTH-008, HTH-010, and HTH-011. These should reuse the same decision modules, compatibility labels, source notes, and safety patterns.

### Wave 3 — Add proof and personality

Produce HTH-012 through HTH-017 only after field evidence exists. Gear Verdicts cannot be generated from product pages. Field Reports require privacy review and delayed publication.

### Wave 4 — Add place carefully

Publish HTH-018 after access, regulation, stewardship, and location-privacy treatment have been validated. The first Water Profile should be substantial enough to justify a real archive destination.

## 8. Internal-link topology

The launch library should form deliberate reader paths rather than a flat post grid.

- HTH-001 routes to HTH-003, HTH-006, HTH-009, HTH-014, and HTH-016.
- Every Water Read routes to one Technique, one Equipment System, and the relevant Safety/Regulation page.
- Every Technique routes to required equipment relationships, common failure modes, and one Field Report when evidence exists.
- Every Equipment System routes to Gear Verdicts only after the general decision is answered.
- Gear Verdicts route back to system compatibility and include a no-purchase alternative.
- Species Profiles route to Water Reads, Techniques, handling/stewardship guidance, and official regulation authorities.
- Field Reports route to the question being tested, not merely to “related posts.”
- The Water Profile routes to current official access/regulation sources and relevant species/technique content.
- Cross-publication links appear only after the fishing answer is complete and only when the next task is legitimate.

## 10. Required content modules

Each format should use only the modules that perform a real job:

- **Field Report:** timestamp, conditions, observation, limitation, next question.
- **Verdict:** reader problem, evidence basis, best fit, limitations, compatibility, alternatives, affiliate status.
- **Compatibility Matrix:** required, compatible, conditional, incompatible, with rationale.
- **Regulation Warning:** jurisdiction, responsible authority, review date, limitation, confirmation instruction.
- **Safety Note:** immediate hazard, preparation, and emergency action.
- **Source Note:** evidence tier, source type, last reviewed, corrections, and unknowns.
- **Location Privacy Indicator:** editor-only classification; private values never enter public output.

## 12. Definition of ready

A brief is ready for drafting only when it contains:

- One reader problem and one promised outcome.
- Content family, pillar, and required structured fields.
- First-hand evidence available or explicitly absent.
- Source plan and required official authority.
- Privacy, safety, regulation, affiliate, and contributor gates.
- Required visual evidence or diagram.
- Internal-link entry and exit routes.
- Last-reviewed and refresh triggers.

## 13. Definition of publishable

A piece is publishable only when:

- The primary fishing answer is complete and visible early.
- Observation, inference, sourced fact, and recommendation are distinguishable.
- The contributor’s experience is neither hidden nor overstated.
- Dates, conditions, limitations, alternatives, and compatibility are recorded where relevant.
- Regulation and access information includes jurisdiction, authority, and review date.
- Location privacy is applied to text, images, metadata, feeds, schema, and REST output.
- Disclosure appears before commercial paths.
- The page has no fake destination, placeholder archive, or unsupported expertise claim.
- Mobile, keyboard, screen-reader, contrast, print, and reduced-motion checks pass.
