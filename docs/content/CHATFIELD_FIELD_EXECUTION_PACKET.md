## Closure-check process

Run the following sequence before travel, again before entering the park, and immediately before publication:

1. Check the official Chatfield State Park page and closure notices.
2. Check current park hours, fees, access restrictions, fire restrictions, recovery or emergency closures, and tree-removal closures.
3. Check the current Colorado fishing regulation brochure and any emergency regulations.
4. Check boat-ramp and aquatic nuisance species inspection requirements if using any watercraft.
5. Check current weather, wind, lightning risk, air quality, and heat exposure.
6. Record the source URL, timestamp, finding, reviewer, and go/no-go decision.
7. Stop the session if the official source is unavailable and the uncertainty affects lawful access or safety.
8. Repeat the verification immediately before publishing any access, fee, closure, regulation, or condition statement.

## Session 1 protocol

**Working assignment:** Chatfield Reservoir — morning wind and light shift — smallmouth bass.

**Test question:** As wind and light change during the morning, what should change first: productive depth, shoreline angle, retrieve speed, or presentation?

Required observations:

- Date and exact session window
- Access mode and generalized zone only
- Air and water temperature when available
- Wind speed/direction and change points
- Light/cloud state and change points
- Water clarity and visible depth estimate
- Depth band and structural class
- Exact rod, reel, line, leader, knot, lure, weight, and hook
- Retrieve cadence, angle, contact, and changes
- Cast count or effort proxy
- Fish contacts, landed fish, misses, follows, and failures
- Handling time and release condition
- What failed, what remained unknown, and the next test

No precise catch coordinates, live location, or sensitive structure disclosure may enter public output.

## Repeat-session protocol

Use the same water, target species, core setup, access mode, and approximately comparable session window. Change exactly one planned variable:

1. Leader length, or
2. Lure weight, or
3. Retrieve speed.

Weather, water level, clarity, wind, light, fishing pressure, and temperature are uncontrolled variables and must be recorded rather than treated as equivalent.

Two sessions may support a comparison narrative. They may not establish a universal rule.

## Smallmouth-bass evidence pack scope

The evidence pack must distinguish:

- Species identification and possible confusion risks
- Native/non-native status by jurisdiction
- Habitat and structure use
- Seasonal movement
- Temperature and dissolved-oxygen context
- Feeding ecology and prey relationships
- Spawning and handling sensitivity
- Colorado regulations and water-specific exceptions
- Ethical handling and release limitations
- Claims supported by official management records versus general biological literature
- Field observations versus sourced facts

Preferred source hierarchy:

1. Colorado Parks and Wildlife regulations, fishery surveys, park records, and species material
2. USGS, USFWS, state/federal reservoir operators, and peer-reviewed fisheries research
3. Manufacturer specifications only for exact equipment specifications
4. Field observations, clearly dated and bounded

## Gear Verdict completion gates

### HTH-012 — Reel

Must contain:

- Exact manufacturer, model, size, and variant
- Ownership and use dates
- Rod, line, leader, lure-weight, and technique context
- Minimum session count or explicit shorter-test disclosure
- Drag behavior, line management, wind performance, retrieve feel, balance, maintenance, wear, failures, and alternatives
- Non-purchase option
- Disclosure and last-reviewed date

### HTH-013 — Braid and leader

Must contain:

- Exact braid and fluorocarbon products
- Rated strengths and verified diameters when available
- Spool age and storage history
- Knot, leader length, guide passage, wind behavior, abrasion, retie frequency, break-offs, visibility context, lure range, and limitations
- No laboratory-strength inference from field use
- Non-purchase option
- Disclosure and last-reviewed date
