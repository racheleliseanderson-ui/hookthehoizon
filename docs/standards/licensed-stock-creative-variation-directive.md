# Licensed Stock, Creative Variation, and Controlled Mischief Directive

Date: 2026-07-23
Status: Controlling ecosystem direction

## Scope

This directive applies across the complete five-publication editorial ecosystem and Claire Elise Wellness as a separate, equal-priority brand, product, application, website, and future service environment.

## Licensed Adobe Stock authority

Adobe Stock assets supplied for these projects are approved, properly licensed project assets under an active subscription. They must not be rejected, quarantined, downgraded, or treated as uncertain solely because they are stock images. Where an asset is otherwise suitable, it should be treated as a primary approved source rather than a temporary placeholder.

Unattached status is an implementation state, not a rejection state. Existing licensed assets must be reviewed for intentional placement across pages, posts, products, applications, campaigns, email, social, print, and future website development.

## Creative variation requirement

The visual system must avoid becoming overly polished, predictable, sterile, generic, or uniformly serious. Each site must include carefully placed moments of humor, surprise, mischief, visual contrast, interruption, and occasional controlled oddness where these improve the experience without weakening clarity, accessibility, trust, safety, evidence standards, or individual publication identity.

Required behavior:

- Introduce publication-specific wit, playful captions, unexpected imagery, visual interruption, dynamic composition, and restrained off-brand energy where appropriate.
- Vary image scale, page pacing, alignment, density, sectional treatment, and visual rhythm so templates do not produce repetitive or interchangeable experiences.
- Do not force humor into every component, but do not allow the default system to drift toward dull, generic, or excessively restrained presentation.
- Preserve the distinct voice and visual identity of each publication and Claire Elise Wellness. Mischief is contextual and brand-specific, not a universal overlay.
- Continue to use custom, generated, illustrated, or unusual imagery when the licensed library cannot fulfill a specific creative job.

## Media governance

Media classification and asset registers must distinguish:

- license/source status
- editorial suitability
- route and application fit
- crop and orientation fit
- accessibility metadata
- creative role
- AI disclosure where applicable
- duplicate or near-duplicate status
- safety, depiction, factual, or location-sensitivity flags

Stock provenance alone is not a negative review flag. Appropriate review flags may still apply for factual mismatch, safety, depiction risk, redundancy, unsuitable tone, weak crop behavior, or route conflict.

## Acceptance criteria

A completed page, template, application, campaign, or product experience must be both functional and authored. Technically correct but visually repetitive, generic, sterile, or excessively restrained work may fail review.

The target portfolio must remain useful and credible while also feeling dynamic, surprising, memorable, human, and occasionally mischievous.
