# Hook the Horizon — Object Descriptor and Closed Inventory Standard

**Status:** Active mandatory visual-production rule  
**Date:** 2026-07-17  
**Scope:** AI-generated and AI-assisted photography, gear studies, rig illustrations, field setups, destination imagery, diagrams, article media, and social derivatives

## Governing rule

If an object can be seen, it must be described, source-locked, or deliberately excluded.

The generator is not the rigging technician, gear editor, field stylist, boat operator, or safety reviewer of record.

Every scene must use a closed inventory. Only listed objects or listed object families may appear. Every unassigned workbench, tackle tray, boat deck, rod holder, pack surface, shoreline zone, garment surface, map field, floor, wall, and background styling area remains clear unless the approved production brief authorizes natural field activity.

Default containment clause:

> Include only the listed movable objects. Keep all unassigned surfaces clear. No unlisted rods, reels, lines, leaders, lures, hooks, terminal tackle, electronics, tools, bags, garments, vessel components, maps, notes, safety equipment, fish-handling items, bottles, packages, or decorative outdoor filler.

## Required object descriptors

Every rod, reel, spool, line, leader, lure, fly, hook, swivel, weight, jig head, terminal-tackle component, electronics unit, mount, battery, tool, bag, garment, footwear item, vessel component, map, note, safety item, fish-handling item, and environmental prop must state:

- exact quantity or approved bounded range;
- identity ID and model or variant when material;
- compatibility, field, safety, or editorial job;
- dimensions, rating, class, or relative scale;
- silhouette, construction, and defining form;
- materials, finish, wear, corrosion, and wet, dry, clean, or used state;
- color role within the approved palette hierarchy;
- exact placement, orientation, spacing, and relationship to adjacent gear;
- line path, knot, connection, or rig sequence where relevant;
- safety and handling state;
- whether it is locked across related images;
- permitted changes and prohibited substitutions.

Repeated equipment may be described as a controlled family only when exact count, shared design grammar, model or class, materials, scale, and placement pattern are specified.

## Generic noun prohibition

The following are insufficient without immediate descriptors:

- fishing gear;
- tackle;
- equipment;
- rods and reels;
- lures or flies;
- terminal tackle;
- electronics;
- rig;
- tools;
- bags;
- technical clothing;
- boat gear;
- safety equipment;
- maps and notes;
- accessories;
- `some`, `several`, `various`, `assorted`, `a few`, or `and other gear`.

A gear image must show a real compatible system, documented test, failure, setup, maintenance task, field decision, or condition. A product pile is not an image job.

## Noun-pass gate

Before generation:

1. Underline every concrete visible noun in Subject, Action / Moment, Environment, and Lighting / Style.
2. Describe it, source-lock it, or remove it.
3. Resolve every collective phrase into an exact inventory or controlled family.
4. Confirm that the containment clause keeps unassigned surfaces clear.

Any unexplained visible noun stops generation.

## Physical and safety logic

Line path, knot sequence, hook placement, rod/reel/line/leader/lure compatibility, orientation, drag and spool state, electronics mounts, vessel location, personal protective equipment, fish handling, current, and water physics must be plausible.

Do not generate:

- extra hooks;
- malformed or discontinuous line paths;
- impossible knots or rigging;
- duplicated reels or equipment;
- floating tackle;
- unsafe deck clutter;
- irrelevant rustic outdoor props;
- false labels or unsupported product marks;
- equipment that cannot function together.

## Source-linked continuity

Image-to-image and related-scene continuity includes:

- object count;
- model or variant;
- dimensions and ratings;
- silhouette;
- materials, finish, and wear;
- line and leader path;
- connection sequence;
- lure and hook identity;
- placement and orientation;
- safety state;
- relationships among objects.

Only additions, removals, replacements, or repositioning listed under **Permitted Changes** may occur.

## Acceptance gate

Reject a candidate when:

- an unlisted prominent object appears;
- a clear surface receives filler;
- the gear forms a random product pile;
- compatibility is wrong;
- product identity changes;
- line or rig logic is impossible;
- handling or deck organization is unsafe;
- repeated equipment loses family logic;
- unrelated rustic, luxury, event, food, beauty, or interior props enter the scene.

A candidate advances only when every visible object has an understandable field, technical, safety, or editorial job; all equipment forms a plausible compatible system; every object matches its approved descriptor; unassigned surfaces remain clear; and the generator invented no filler.
