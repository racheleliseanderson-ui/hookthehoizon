## Operating model

Use a responsible best-effort workflow. Keep a stable Markdown source when useful. Use Drive for substantial editorial work and Notion for status coordination only when each adds practical value.

Cohort and transfer sizes are flexible. Fixed 25–50 article cohorts and fixed 5–10 transfer batches are planning defaults, not completion gates. Separate claim ledgers, duplicate cross-system records, direct confirmation, and exhaustive source packets are optional unless they materially improve the item.

Continue safe research, drafting, editing, testing, and reconciliation when a nonessential integration or record is unavailable. Record only material limitations and apply holds narrowly.

## Review standard

Use credible and sufficiently current support. Distinguish fact from inference and opinion. Preserve Hook the Horizon voice, accessibility, conservation, access, regulation, equipment, water-safety, and sensitive-location boundaries. Do not claim field experience, testing, results, access, approvals, device capability, or verification that did not occur.

Use stronger support for consequential safety, regulation, access, or location claims. Narrow, qualify, reframe, or remove unsupported claims without blocking unrelated work.

## Authorization boundary

Research, drafting, prototyping, previews, and testing may proceed. Publishing, scheduling, destructive overwrite or deletion, disclosure of private or restricted information, and other consequential live actions require specific authorization. WordPress remains draft-only unless the individual item has separate publication approval.

## States

Use only states that help: `planned`, `drafting`, `review`, `revision-required`, `ready`, `wordpress-draft`, `publication-qa`, `published`, or `retired`.
