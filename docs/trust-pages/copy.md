## about

Hook the Horizon follows the water, the setup, and what actually held up.

We help anglers read conditions, prepare intelligently, configure compatible gear, test claims honestly, and learn from what worked, failed, and changed. Premium does not mean a wall of products. It means better judgment, better field notes, clearer limitations, and fewer stories polished past the point of usefulness.

Verified fact, direct observation, contributor observation, hypothesis, inference, and editorial judgment remain distinguishable. A field conclusion states the conditions, duration, setup, limits, and evidence that support it. A product is not identified beyond the available evidence, and a catch or field test is never invented.

Hook the Horizon owns fishing technique, water reading, gear-system compatibility, field reports, honest reviews, trip preparation, stewardship, and angling stories. Honey Hole Intelligence handles bounded planning, current-condition context, regulation freshness, access, conservation, and location-sensitive decisions.

## editorial_standards

Every substantial item identifies the reader decision, conditions, evidence, source status, uncertainty, limitations, and useful next action.

Verified fact, official-source verification, business or authority confirmation, direct observation, contributor observation, desk research, hypothesis, inference, and editorial judgment are not interchangeable. Field reports state where generalization is appropriate without publishing protected specifics. Product conclusions identify the exact setup, conditions, testing duration, compatibility assumptions, failures, and limits. A short or narrow test is not presented as a universal verdict.

Regulations, access, closures, fire restrictions, weather, flows, water conditions, wildlife guidance, and land-management rules are volatile. Material claims use current authoritative evidence, display a review date, and direct readers to verify critical current information. Stewardship and location protection override traffic value.

AI output is not a catch, field test, firsthand experience, product identification, regulation, or access confirmation. Commercial relationships do not determine conclusions. Material safety, regulation, access, conservation, product, or experience errors receive dated correction or withdrawal notes.

## corrections

We correct material errors and update content when field conditions, official information, access, closures, regulations, products, evidence, safety guidance, or circumstances change.

A material correction affects meaning, accuracy, safety, interpretation, recommendation, stewardship, or a decision a reasonable reader might make. Examples include an incorrect regulation or closure, misleading access statement, exposed protected location, wrong product identity, unsupported field-use claim, outdated condition, omitted limitation, or missing commercial relationship.

Material corrections receive a dated note. Significant updates may also change the last-reviewed date. Spelling, formatting, broken links, accessibility repairs, and minor clarifications may be corrected without a formal note when meaning does not change.

Unsupported, unsafe, obsolete, duplicative, privacy-invasive, conservation-harming, or misleading content may be corrected, generalized, redirected, archived, or withdrawn. Reports should use `/contact/#corrections`. Location exposure, serious safety, regulation, access, or conservation concerns receive priority triage.

## privacy

Each enabled feature must state its purpose, processor, retention and deletion rule, and consent behavior. Personal information is not sold as a stand-alone data product.

Before release, the policy needs a working privacy contact, processor and cookie inventory, location and metadata controls, retention and deletion rules, enabled-feature map, consent behavior, and proof that the policy matches the site configuration.

## accessibility

We are working to make Hook the Horizon usable across devices, input methods, vision, hearing, movement, cognition, language, and connection conditions.

The implementation target is WCAG 2.2 Level AA where applicable. Required checks include semantic structure, keyboard operation, visible focus, sufficient contrast, zoom and reflow, reduced motion, text alternatives, accessible forms and errors, readable field tables, and adequate touch targets.

Route, water, weather, compatibility, safety, regulation, access, and condition meaning must not depend on maps, photographs, color, sound, hover, or motion alone. Maps and diagrams need useful text alternatives or generalized descriptions. Compatibility and setup results need logical reading order and printable or text-based versions. Known material limitations are stated honestly with repair status. Do not claim complete conformance without current implementation evidence. Barriers may be reported at `/contact/#accessibility` with the page, problem, device or assistive technology when known, and the intended task.
