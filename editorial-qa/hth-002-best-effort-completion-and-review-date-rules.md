## Completion decision

HTH-002 is complete as a general Safety & Stewardship methodology page. Its purpose is to explain how Hook the Horizon handles changing rules, access, closures, safety, conservation, review dates, official links, uncertainty, and location privacy.

It does **not** require direct agency contact, landowner outreach, permanent certainty, or exhaustive jurisdiction-by-jurisdiction research. Those requirements would be unrealistic for a small editorial operation and are not imposed by the project-wide lean governance standard.

The editorial rule is:

> Find the best reasonably available information, record the responsible source and review date, distinguish fact from inference, disclose what remains unknown, and hold only the specific unsafe or unsupported instruction.

A location-specific article may proceed when it provides:

- a useful description of the water or water type;
- the known management and regulation structure;
- the best available responsible official links;
- material access and safety facts that could be established;
- a review date;
- a same-day or pre-trip verification instruction where conditions can change; and
- a clear statement of unresolved details.

## Unknown is a status, not a pipeline failure

Use these categories:

1. **Confirmed by a responsible public source** — current information appears on an official regulation, agency, land-manager, facility, alert, or data page reviewed on the stated date.
2. **Supported but not controlling** — credible evidence adds context but does not make or enforce the rule.
3. **Unknown or not established** — public information does not establish the detail strongly enough to present it as fact.

Unknown details do not create a project-wide hold. Narrow or remove the affected instruction only when guessing could:

- direct readers onto private or closed land;
- conceal a material license, season, method, inspection, or other legal requirement;
- expose a sensitive or restricted place; or
- create a serious safety risk.

## Official-source architecture

Use the layers that apply to the exact place:

1. State, territorial, or tribal fish and wildlife authority.
2. Land or facility manager: state park, municipality, county, water utility, NPS unit, national forest, BLM office, refuge, tribal authority, private operator, or other identified manager.
3. Official waterbody, park, ramp, or facility page.
4. Current alerts, closure orders, fire restrictions, road notices, wildlife protections, and emergency information.
5. NWS, USCG, local emergency information, and an appropriate USGS monitoring location where relevant.
6. State aquatic-nuisance-species requirements and current conservation guidance.

Public maps and third-party directions may support research but do not independently establish legal access.

## Review-date rules

The displayed review date records when the source was checked. It does not guarantee the information remained unchanged afterward.

### Regulation-year information

Licenses, seasons, species rules, legal methods, bag or possession limits, and water-specific regulations:

- use the current regulation year or season;
- review during drafting or material update;
- recheck when a new regulation year, season, or official correction is issued; and
- direct readers to the current official rules before fishing.

### Routine facility and management information

Permits, inspections, fees, reservations, routine hours, and stable facility rules:

- review during drafting;
- recheck before publication when the review is more than 30 days old or the facility is seasonal; and
- recheck after a reported policy, management, construction, drought, or ownership change.

### Fast-changing access information

Roads, ramps, gates, parking, fire restrictions, temporary closures, and emergency access conditions:

- review as close as practical to publication or update; and
- instruct readers to check the responsible official source the same day or immediately before departure.

### Current conditions

Weather, lightning, water temperature, streamflow, lake level, wildfire, ice, and emergency conditions:

- treat as current-condition information rather than durable article facts;
- timestamp any specific observation;
- recheck immediately before the activity and continue monitoring; and
- never turn a forecast or gauge value into a guarantee of safety.

### Conservation and safety education

General invasive-species, conservation, boating, cold-water, and lightning guidance:

- review official links at least annually and when the responsible authority updates its guidance; and
- apply stricter current state, tribal, land-manager, or water-specific rules when they exist.

### Location privacy

Review before every public release and again whenever images, maps, route files, downloads, structured data, feeds, analytics outputs, or social derivatives are added.

## Current-source evidence for the freshness model

Temporary restrictions are not theoretical. In July 2026, Yellowstone National Park changed selected rivers from partial restrictions to full fishing closures as water temperatures and fish-mortality concerns changed. Colorado Parks and Wildlife also posts water-specific boating closures during the operating season.

These examples support visible review dates and same-day checks. They do not support holding all location content until permanent certainty exists.

## Non-fabrication boundary

This completion status does not claim:

- direct agency or landowner contact;
- permission through private land;
- a site visit;
- a verified informal route, crossing, bank, launch, trail, or parking location;
- safe conditions;
- complete regulation coverage;
- professional legal, medical, emergency, boating, conservation, or safety review;
- field testing, catches, equipment ownership, or undocumented contributor experience; or
- authorization to expose a sensitive location.
